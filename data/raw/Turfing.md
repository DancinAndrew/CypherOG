**Turfing** (or **turf dancing**) is a form of [street dance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Street_dance "Street dance") that originated in [Oakland, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oakland,_California "Oakland, California"), United States, characterized by rhythmic movement combined with waving, floor moves, gliding, flexing, and contortioning. It was developed by youth from West Oakland and organized by dancer Jeriel Bey, who coined the name "turf dancing," or "Turfin" and named his Organization The Architeckz™. Bey named the dance form as an [acronym](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acronym "Acronym") for "Taking Up Room on the Floor."<sup id="cite_ref-Burkey_1-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-Burkey-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Zamora_2-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-Zamora-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-stronach_3-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-stronach-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> The style was originally known by the terms "having fun with it" or "hitting it", but these names didn't seem marketable. However, another claim for the nomenclature considers the acronym as a [backronym](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Backronym "Backronym") and that _turf dancing_ originated as a way to describe dances that different "turfs" (locations or territories) from Oakland performed to represent where they were from (the same as "blocks" or "sets"). The dance form had its earliest influences in the [Boogaloo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boogaloo_(freestyle_dance) "Boogaloo (freestyle dance)") movement of the mid-1960s, but it developed into a distinctive dance style.<sup id="cite_ref-phan_4-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-phan-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup>

The dance form can be traced back to the traditions of the [Boogaloo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boogaloo_(funk_dance) "Boogaloo (funk dance)") movement in Oakland, California in the 1960s,<sup id="cite_ref-phan_4-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-phan-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Zamora_2-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-Zamora-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> developing into a separate genre of dance in the 1990s. Along with [hyphy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyphy "Hyphy") music, it came to be seen as distinctively representative of Oakland.<sup id="cite_ref-Hix_5-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-Hix-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The movement rose to prominence in 2002 following Jeriel Bey's establishment of his group, "The Architeckz™."

Turfing is a social critique and recognition of lost African American lives, police brutality, and race relations in Oakland.<sup id="cite_ref-6"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> This form of expression uniquely addresses African American deaths, while using art to create a politicized awareness of brutality against people of color.<sup id="cite_ref-7"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> It is also seen as a means to humanize blackness, spread community, empathy, and peace in the same streets black lives were taken.<sup id="cite_ref-9"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> Apart from being a unique hood dance form, turfing represents a collective movement, and is part of the street culture in the [East Bay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Bay_(San_Francisco_Bay_Area) "East Bay (San Francisco Bay Area)").<sup id="cite_ref-10"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup>

Turf dancing was first displayed on videos from artists such as [Baby Bash](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baby_Bash "Baby Bash"), [Keak Da Sneak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keak_Da_Sneak "Keak Da Sneak"), and [E-40](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/E-40 "E-40") by innovative and influential turf dancers (and creators of the turfing Lifestyle) Jeriel Bey.<sup id="cite_ref-Hix_5-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-Hix-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Turf dance had been promoted as a means of [dispute resolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dispute_resolution "Dispute resolution") within the Oakland community,<sup id="cite_ref-Hix_5-2"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-Hix-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> and in 2005 The Architeckz built on this concept of dance battle by engaging [krump](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Krump "Krump") dancers from [Los Angeles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Los_Angeles "Los Angeles") in city-level competition.<sup id="cite_ref-Burkey_1-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-Burkey-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Friendly rivalries with dancers from [New York City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City "New York City") and [Memphis, Tennessee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memphis,_Tennessee "Memphis, Tennessee"),<sup id="cite_ref-Zamora_2-2"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-Zamora-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> have developed as well. The artist [E-40](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/E-40 "E-40") brought international attention to the movement with his 2006 single "[Tell Me When to Go](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tell_Me_When_to_Go "Tell Me When to Go")".<sup id="cite_ref-12"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2012 Alonzo (Turf) Jones<sup id="cite_ref-13"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> a.k.a. Retro, a turf dancer and skateboarder from California's Bay Area competed on the TV talent competition _[America's Got Talent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/America%27s_Got_Talent "America's Got Talent")_ [in season 7](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/America%27s_Got_Talent_(season_7) "America's Got Talent (season 7)").<sup id="cite_ref-14"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup>

The foundation of turf dance is based on a series of dances with different "turfs" in Oakland displayed. Some of these foundation dances are "two step," "brookfield," "auntie," "shaking," "the busta," "cliffhangers," "tsunamis" and "traces". Turf dance also includes "story telling" which is a style based on pantomime "life stories" or "everyday activities". Turfing incorporates other dance style concepts such as tutting, flexing, waving and animation. Turf dance is an improvisational, free-flowing form of dance that is based on the idea of pure cause and effect but focuses on storytelling, the creation of [optical illusions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optical_illusions "Optical illusions"), and the display of distinctive personal style. Dance elements include [Boogaloo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boogaloo_(freestyle_dance) "Boogaloo (freestyle dance)"), [popping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popping "Popping"), [locking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Locking_(dance) "Locking (dance)"), and [miming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mime "Mime").<sup id="cite_ref-15"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Gliding is heavily used in turf dance because it enables the creation of optical illusions. "Going dumb," or completely letting one's emotions loose on the dance floor, is also a distinctive part of the tradition.<sup id="cite_ref-16"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turfing#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup>
