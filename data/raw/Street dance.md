**Street dance** is an umbrella term for a large number of social dance styles such as: [breakdancing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Breakdancing "Breakdancing"), [popping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popping "Popping"), [locking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Locking_(dance) "Locking (dance)"), [house dance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_dance "House dance"), [waacking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waacking "Waacking"), [voguing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voguing "Voguing"), [Hip Hop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hip_Hop "Hip Hop"), etc. Social dance styles have many accompanying steps and foundations, created organically from a culture, a moment in time, a way of life, influenced by natural social interaction.<sup id="cite_ref-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Street_dance#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> A street dance is a [vernacular dance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vernacular_dance "Vernacular dance") in an urban context.<sup id="cite_ref-2"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Street_dance#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Vernacular dances are often [improvisational](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Improvisation "Improvisation") and [social](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_dance "Social dance") in nature, encouraging interaction and contact with spectators and other dancers. These dances are a part of the [vernacular culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vernacular_culture "Vernacular culture") of the geographical area that they come from.

Street dance evolved during the 1970s outside [dance studios](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dance_studio "Dance studio") in any available open space. This includes streets, dance parties, [block parties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Block_parties "Block parties"), parks, school yards, [raves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rave "Rave"), and nightclubs. This is partly because African Americans who created the style were generally not accepted into dance studios because of their race. A significant feature of street dance is that it was historically a dance of young people offering a sense of freedom and giving a 'voice' to unrepresented communities.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Street_dance#cite_note-:0-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup>

Due to the mix of styles within street dance, its origin can not be traced to one precise area, however, America is the birthplace of most street styles. [Breakdance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Breakdancing "Breakdancing") originated in the [Bronx](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Bronx "The Bronx") and [Lite Feet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Litefeet "Litefeet") emerged in Harlem, both in [New York City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City "New York City"),<sup id="cite_ref-4"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Street_dance#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Street_dance#cite_note-:0-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Street_dance#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> while popping and locking developed during the 1970s funk era in [California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California "California"), USA. [Clogging](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clogging "Clogging"), which evolved in the streets and factories of [Northern England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_England "Northern England") in the mid-19th century, is an early form of street dance.<sup id="cite_ref-6"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Street_dance#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup>
