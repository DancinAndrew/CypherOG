## ORGANIZATION FOR DANCERS AND MORE!

  
Infinite Force, headquartered in Gdańsk, is recognized as one of the leading dance organizations on the international stage, distinguished by its innovation and passion for dance. We take pride in creating some of the largest dance events in the world, which significantly shape the global dance scene.

  
For over a decade, Infinite Force has been a symbol of quality and professionalism. With authenticity, extensive experience, and a strategic approach, we continuously develop and promote dance culture, shaping global trends. Our aim is to set new standards in the world of dance, inspire current and future generations of dancers, and develop an integrated dance community on an international scale.

Our key events annually gather hundreds of dancers, enthusiasts and renowned artists from all over the world, including Europe, Korea, Japan, China, and the United States. Infinite Force has a significant impact both on the international dance scene and locally in Poland. We are actively engaged in dance education, promoting dance and building intercultural bridges, which attracts and develops our global community. Infinite Force represents the infinite power of dance, boundless possibilities, emotions, people, and a place where tradition harmoniously blends with modernity. We create a space where dancers of all skill levels can find opportunities for development, self-expression, and the realization of their dance ambitions.

#### infinite force camp 2024

Gdańsk / Poland

## 11-18.08  
2024

The project was partially financed by the Krajowy Plan Odbudowy i Zwiększania Odporności (KPO), Investment A2.5.1, under Agreement No. 1375KPO.GRANTYNIMiT2024.  
The international dance festival Gdańsk Dance Week: Infinite Force Camp 2024 took place from August 11 to 18, 2024 (on-site), and continued until October 18, 2024 (online). The event combined a passion for dance, recreation, and education with modern technologies, including a mobile application powered by artificial intelligence. The festival featured 16 outstanding artists from Poland and abroad. The program consisted of four segments: workshop, recreational, competition, and virtual.  
All of the intended goals were achieved: international cultural and artistic exchange was promoted, participants' dance skills were enhanced, the dance community was integrated, and attention was drawn to the importance of dance and ecology in artistic activities. The project also strengthened participants' digital and ecological competencies through the use of modern technological tools and the implementation of an eco-friendly policy.  
The event engaged over 200 participants in workshops, more than 500 in the dance competition, and the live streams attracted over 10,000 viewers in total. The mobile application and social media activity contributed to an impressive total of 10,000,000 views.  
The project increased the resilience of the Applicant and the entire cultural sector to potential economic crises by diversifying activities towards online engagement—through both the mobile application and social media. The festival demonstrated strong development and organizational potential, continuously increasing its level of professionalism year by year. The modern event infrastructure and consistent commitment to pro-environmental initiatives provided a solid foundation for the long-term and sustainable development of the project.
