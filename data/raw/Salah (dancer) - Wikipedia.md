**Salah Benlemqawanssa** (born June 28, 1979), also known as **Salah the Entertainer** and **Spider Salah**, is a competitive [hip-hop dancer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hip-hop_dance "Hip-hop dance") from France who won the inaugural season of _[La France a un incroyable talent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_France_a_un_incroyable_talent "La France a un incroyable talent")_ (_France has Incredible Talent_), the fourth season of _[Arabs Got Talent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabs_Got_Talent "Arabs Got Talent")_ and the fourth season of _[Tú Sí Que Vales (Italy)](https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/T%C3%BA_s%C3%AD_que_vales_(Italia) "it:Tú sí que vales (Italia)")_. He was born in the Paris suburb [Saint-Denis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saint-Denis,_Seine-Saint-Denis "Saint-Denis, Seine-Saint-Denis") and is of Moroccan (Father) and Algerian (Mother) ancestry.<sup id="cite_ref-sport_2-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-sport-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-agt_3-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-agt-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> He calls his personal dance style P.A.B.E. which stands for [Popping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popping "Popping"), [Animation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popping#Related_styles_and_techniques "Popping"), [Boogaloo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_boogaloo_(dance) "Electric boogaloo (dance)"), and Effects.<sup id="cite_ref-BWD_1-2"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-BWD-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Although Salah is best known for popping, he is also skilled in [b-boying](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/B-boying "B-boying").

Salah started dancing in 1996 after he saw the [dance crew](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dance_crew "Dance crew") O Posse practicing their moves in front of the mirrors of the [Théâtre national de Chaillot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Th%C3%A9%C3%A2tre_national_de_Chaillot "Théâtre national de Chaillot").<sup id="cite_ref-interview_8-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-interview-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> Two years later, he and his crew The Family won second place at the international b-boying competition [Battle of the Year](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_the_Year "Battle of the Year") (BOTY) in 1998. In 1999, he joined the [contemporary dance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contemporary_dance "Contemporary dance") company Montalvo-Hervieu.<sup id="cite_ref-montherv_9-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-montherv-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup>

In 2006, Salah won the first season of _Incroyable Talent_.<sup id="cite_ref-beats_10-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-beats-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ng_11-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-ng-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2007, he performed a one-man stage show at London-based [Breakin' Convention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Breakin%27_Convention "Breakin' Convention") called "The Dream of Gluby".<sup id="cite_ref-breakcon07_12-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-breakcon07-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2008, he starred as himself in _Beats Per Minute_, an independent film about a French popper who discovers he can rewind time with his dance moves.<sup id="cite_ref-13"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2009, Salah served as a judge at BOTY's international one-on-one [b-boy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/B-boy "B-boy") battle in [Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Germany "Germany"). BOTY is a b-boy competition exclusively for b-boy crews; however, to celebrate their 20th anniversary they had a competition for b-boy solo dancers.

> "My life is dance and dance is my life. I sacrifice a lot for the dance. I am always practicing or performing. There is not much time for a personal life, but I love what I do! It takes a lot of inspiration, time and dedication to become a good dancer."

Salah<sup id="cite_ref-interview_8-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-interview-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup>

In 2011, Salah led a digital project called Breathe the Beat. Through Breathe the Beat, he released a series of five video tutorials explaining how he takes inspiration from the world around him to create his P.A.B.E. dance style.<sup id="cite_ref-14"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> He also judged an online competition based on the principles of his tutorials to see which dancer could create the best one-minute performance. The winner, chosen by Salah, that created the best routine was James "AnimatedJ" Jimenez from the USA.<sup id="cite_ref-15"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> The project also featured a series of roadshows around the UK taught by Brooke and Roxy from Funkstylerz and Plague dance crews.<sup id="cite_ref-16"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> After Breath the Beat, Salah toured North America from October 2011 to October 2012 as a featured dancer in [Cirque du Soleil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cirque_du_Soleil "Cirque du Soleil")'s production _[Michael Jackson: The Immortal World Tour](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Jackson:_The_Immortal_World_Tour "Michael Jackson: The Immortal World Tour")_.<sup id="cite_ref-cirquemj_17-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-cirquemj-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup>

In 2013, Salah became a brand ambassador for [Puma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puma_SE "Puma SE"). Through this partnership, Puma launched a dance project called "Puma the Quest" that gave five dancers the opportunity to travel the world and be personally mentored by Salah. The street dancers were chosen from hundreds of video submissions which were narrowed down to a core group of 20. The core group had to perform live in front of a panel of judges including singer Nawell Madani, rapper [Youssoupha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Youssoupha "Youssoupha"), and Salah himself. The five winners—Lara Laquiz, Tiet Sofian, Steph 2SL, Anto, and b-boy Ska—were announced the following day at the [Juste Debout](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Juste_Debout&action=edit&redlink=1 "Juste Debout (page does not exist)") 2013 finals.<sup id="cite_ref-18"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-movement_19-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-movement-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> They traveled with Salah to New York City, Berlin, Tokyo, Paris, London, and Los Angeles to learn about the street culture within each city. When they returned to Paris they created a show about their experience around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-movement_19-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-movement-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup>

Salah specializes in hip-hop, but he has also studied other forms of dance including [African](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_dance "African dance"), [salsa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salsa_(dance) "Salsa (dance)"), [tap](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tap_dance "Tap dance"), [contemporary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contemporary_dance "Contemporary dance"), and [belly dancing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belly_dancing "Belly dancing").<sup id="cite_ref-interview_8-3"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-interview-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> Salah is a member of Vagabonds<sup id="cite_ref-BWD_1-4"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-BWD-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> and [Massive Monkees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massive_Monkees "Massive Monkees") b-boy crews and Montalvo-Hervieu contemporary dance company.<sup id="cite_ref-montherv_9-5"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-montherv-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> To stay in shape, he works out five times a week with two trainers.<sup id="cite_ref-sport_2-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_note-sport-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup>

1.  **[^](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_ref-25 "Jump up")** He won the world b-boy crew battle with Vagabonds.
2.  **[^](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_ref-30 "Jump up")** The official name of _La France a un incroyable talent_ in the first season was _Incroyable Talent_.
3.  **[^](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah_(dancer)#cite_ref-36 "Jump up")** He judged the locking and popping competitions only.
