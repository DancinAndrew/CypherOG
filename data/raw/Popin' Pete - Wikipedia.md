**Timothy Earl Solomon** (born August 5, 1961), known as **Popin' Pete**, is an American dancer and choreographer who popularized the "[popping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popping "Popping")" dance style and member of the [Electric Boogaloos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_Boogaloos "Electric Boogaloos"). Pete’s career has spanned over forty years since the emergence of popping dance.<sup id="cite_ref-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popin%27_Pete#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup>

Being inspired by his older brother, “Boogaloo” Sam Solomon, who by that time had already mastered all existing street dance styles, Pete would always watch Sam dance and learn from him . Around the years 1975-1976 Sam would combine the local [popping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popping "Popping") techniques & [Boogaloo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boogaloo_(funk_dance) "Boogaloo (funk dance)") styles that originated in Oakland.<sup id="cite_ref-2"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popin%27_Pete#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popin%27_Pete#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> He compiled a set of basic movements and founded [The Electric Boogaloos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Electric_Boogaloos "The Electric Boogaloos").

### The Electric Boogaloos

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Popin%27_Pete&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: The Electric Boogaloos")\]

Popin’ Pete is a first generation member of [The Electric Boogaloos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Electric_Boogaloos "The Electric Boogaloos"), joining the group in 1978, Pete began to learn the [popping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popping "Popping") style. According to Pete, he learned to pop first because [Boogaloo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boogaloo_(funk_dance) "Boogaloo (funk dance)") was too difficult. Boogaloo Sam<sup id="cite_ref-4"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popin%27_Pete#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> as a creator of the group taught basics to the members of The Electric Boogaloos. As all the members were very experienced and passionate dancers, the group soon became successful. During that time, Pete and the other members of The Electric Boogaloos appeared on many popular shows including: [_The Midnight Special_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Midnight_Special_(TV_series) "The Midnight Special (TV series)"), _Kicks_ and _[Soul Train](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soul_Train "Soul Train")_ in the United States. (firstly in 1980<sup id="cite_ref-5"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popin%27_Pete#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup>).

### Relationship with Michael Jackson

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Popin%27_Pete&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Relationship with Michael Jackson")\]

In 1982, [Michael Jackson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Jackson "Michael Jackson"), after seeing their _[Soul Train](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soul_Train "Soul Train")_ performance, approached members of [The Electric Boogaloos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Electric_Boogaloos "The Electric Boogaloos") to choreograph both the "[Thriller](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Jackson%27s_Thriller_(music_video) "Michael Jackson's Thriller (music video)")" and "[Beat It](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beat_It "Beat It")" videos.<sup id="cite_ref-BreakinConvention_6-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popin%27_Pete#cite_note-BreakinConvention-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Their relationship with Jackson spanned almost two decades working in various roles — from numerous music videos (e.g. "Beat It", _[Captain EO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Captain_EO "Captain EO")_ & _[Michael Jackson's Ghosts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Jackson%27s_Ghosts "Michael Jackson's Ghosts")_) to multimedia movies, live performances, choreography, and lessons.<sup id="cite_ref-7"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popin%27_Pete#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup>

### Popin’ Pete in funk culture

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Popin%27_Pete&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Popin’ Pete in funk culture")\]

Popin Pete has gone on to innovate styles/moves like crazy legs, ET, spider man and sleepy style. With [Electric Boogaloos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_Boogaloos "Electric Boogaloos") he performs on the most authoritative scenes spreading American funkstyles culture all over the world,<sup id="cite_ref-BreakinConvention_6-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popin%27_Pete#cite_note-BreakinConvention-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> working alongside some of this generation's most dedicated and hard working music artists. Artists such as: [Justin Timberlake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Justin_Timberlake "Justin Timberlake"), [Gwen Stefani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gwen_Stefani "Gwen Stefani"), [Mýa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%BDa "Mýa"), [The Black Eyed Peas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Black_Eyed_Peas "The Black Eyed Peas") and [Janet Jackson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Janet_Jackson "Janet Jackson") etc.  
For 35 years Popin Pete and the Electric Boogaloos have been developing funk styles in the world. Popin Pete is invited as a judge to the dance competitions ("battles") in United Kingdom (UK Bboy Championship,<sup id="cite_ref-9"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popin%27_Pete#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> Breakin' Convention<sup id="cite_ref-10"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popin%27_Pete#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup>), [Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europe "Europe"), Japan (Dance Delight Japan,<sup id="cite_ref-11"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popin%27_Pete#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> Old School Night<sup id="cite_ref-12"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popin%27_Pete#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup>), [South Korea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Korea "South Korea"), China (Keep on dancing<sup id="cite_ref-13"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popin%27_Pete#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup>) etc. He is considered to be influential figure in street-dance culture.<sup id="cite_ref-14"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popin%27_Pete#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> His students from the United States, Canada, UK, [Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europe "Europe"), [Brazil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brazil "Brazil"), Japan, China, [South Korea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Korea "South Korea"), Russia call him "living legend".<sup id="cite_ref-15"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popin%27_Pete#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> On January 25, 2012 the [Electric
