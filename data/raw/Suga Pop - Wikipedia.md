**Steve da Silva'**, better known as **Suga Pop**,<sup id="cite_ref-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suga_Pop#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> is an American "[street dance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Street_dance "Street dance")" practitioner and choreographer based in the [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "United States"). He is known for his work in "popping" and "locking", styles of dance collectively grouped under the umbrella term "[funk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Funk "Funk") styles". These styles are associated with the U.S. West Coast, particularly California. He has been affiliated with the performance groups [Electric Boogaloos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_Boogaloos "Electric Boogaloos") and [Rock Steady Crew](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rock_Steady_Crew "Rock Steady Crew").<sup id="cite_ref-auto_2-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suga_Pop#cite_note-auto-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup>

Suga Pop worked with artists such as [Michael Jackson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Jackson "Michael Jackson"), [James Brown](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Brown "James Brown"), [Lionel Richie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lionel_Richie "Lionel Richie"), [Janet Jackson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Janet_Jackson "Janet Jackson"), and [Sheila E](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sheila_E "Sheila E") in the 1980s and 1990s, touring as a dancer, featuring in televised performances such as [Herbie Hancock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herbie_Hancock "Herbie Hancock")'s performance of "[Rockit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rockit_(instrumental) "Rockit (instrumental)")" at the 1984 [Grammy Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grammy_Awards "Grammy Awards"),<sup id="cite_ref-3"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suga_Pop#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> and appearing in such seminal pop musical videos as Michael Jackson's "[Beat It](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beat_It "Beat It")" and "[Thriller](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thriller_(song) "Thriller (song)")," and Janet Jackson's "[That's The Way Love Goes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/That%27s_the_Way_Love_Goes_(Janet_Jackson_song) "That's the Way Love Goes (Janet Jackson song)")." After touring as a dancer with Lionel Richie, and then with Sheila E in support of [Prince](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prince_(musician) "Prince (musician)")'s [Purple Rain Tour](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Purple_Rain_Tour "Purple Rain Tour") of 1984–1985,<sup id="cite_ref-4"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suga_Pop#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Suga Pop ventured into music collaboration and production. He has subsequently played live with such acts as [A Tribe Called Quest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Tribe_Called_Quest "A Tribe Called Quest"), [De La Soul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/De_La_Soul "De La Soul"), Amp Fiddler, [Cypress Hill](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cypress_Hill "Cypress Hill"), [Brand Nubian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brand_Nubian "Brand Nubian"), [LL Cool J](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LL_Cool_J "LL Cool J"), [Third Bass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Third_Bass "Third Bass"), and the [Boo-Yaa T.R.I.B.E.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boo-Yaa_T.R.I.B.E. "Boo-Yaa T.R.I.B.E."), in addition to recording and releasing his own material under the band name Pop's Cool Love, and mostly recently, under the name Pop. A video for the single "My Suga" was loaded to YouTube in 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-5"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suga_Pop#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> and the single is available on iTunes.

Suga Pop is recognized as a key contributor to the international street dancing scene, regularly teaching and delivering workshops on the dance forms of [locking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Locking_(dance) "Locking (dance)") and [popping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popping "Popping") around the world. He is recognized by other dance practitioners as playing a key role in teaching these West Coast forms to dancers in New York in the early 1980s, and in subsequently teaching the New York street dance form b-boying or breaking in Los Angeles, after returning from the East Coast.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_2-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suga_Pop#cite_note-auto-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup>
