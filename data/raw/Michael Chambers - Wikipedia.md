**Michael "Boogaloo Shrimp" Chambers** (born November 13, 1967) is an [American](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Americans "Americans") [dancer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dancer "Dancer") and [actor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Actor "Actor"), known for his role as "Turbo" in the 1984 film _[Breakin'](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Breakin%27 "Breakin'")_ and its sequel, _[Breakin' 2: Electric Boogaloo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Breakin%27_2:_Electric_Boogaloo "Breakin' 2: Electric Boogaloo")_, in which he is credited as "Boogaloo Shrimp". Chambers, along with his _Breakin'_ series co-star [Adolfo "Shabba Doo" Quiñones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shabba_Doo "Shabba Doo") and other dancers from the films, were prominently featured in the music videos for [Lionel Richie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lionel_Richie "Lionel Richie")'s "[All Night Long](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/All_Night_Long_(All_Night) "All Night Long (All Night)")" (1983) and [Chaka Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chaka_Khan "Chaka Khan")'s "[I Feel for You](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/I_Feel_for_You "I Feel for You")" (1984).

Chambers, born in [Wilmington, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wilmington,_Los_Angeles "Wilmington, Los Angeles"), is the youngest of four. In 1978, while at junior high, Chambers saw a member of the Samoan American dance group Blue City Strutters perform. The group would influence Chambers' style by performing King Tut and domino routines.<sup id="cite_ref-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Chambers#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Initially, he formulated his style of dance through his interest in fantasy and sci-fi television shows, including the work of [Ray Harryhausen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ray_Harryhausen "Ray Harryhausen") and others. He credits his older brother with introducing him to the "[moonwalk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moonwalk_(dance) "Moonwalk (dance)")".

While still a teenager, Chambers' older brother would take him to [Redondo Beach, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Redondo_Beach,_California "Redondo Beach, California"), where he would perform for money.<sup>[<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Chambers' sister gave him a jacket that read "Mike a.k.a. Boogaloo Shrimp", his street dancing name. Chambers would meet Bruno "Pop N Taco" Falcon and Timothy "[Popin' Pete](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popin%27_Pete "Popin' Pete")". Both would later be featured in the _Breakin'_ series.

Chambers' early stints were through television commercials, followed by an appearance in the music video for Lionel Richie's "All Night Long". He made appearances in the videos for Chaka Khan's "I Feel For You", [Richard Marx](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Marx "Richard Marx")' "[Children of the Night](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Children_of_the_Night_(Richard_Marx_song) "Children of the Night (Richard Marx song)")", [Stacey Q](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stacey_Q "Stacey Q")'s "[Give You All My Love](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Give_You_All_My_Love "Give You All My Love")" and [Paula Abdul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paula_Abdul "Paula Abdul")'s "[Opposites Attract](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Opposites_Attract "Opposites Attract")", where he played the character [MC Skat Kat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MC_Skat_Kat "MC Skat Kat") and was an assistant choreographer. In 1985, he co-starred in "[Stop the Madness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stop_the_Madness "Stop the Madness")", an anti-drug music video sponsored by the [Reagan administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reagan_administration "Reagan administration"). He also appeared in such films as _[Bill & Ted's Bogus Journey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bill_%26_Ted%27s_Bogus_Journey "Bill & Ted's Bogus Journey")_ (1991)—as a dancing robot—_[Naked Gun 33+1⁄3: The Final Insult](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naked_Gun_33_1/3:_The_Final_Insult "Naked Gun 33 1/3: The Final Insult")_ (1994) and _[Dudley Do-Right](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dudley_Do-Right_(film) "Dudley Do-Right (film)")_ (1999). He also appeared as [Urkel-Bot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steve_Urkel#Inventions "Steve Urkel") in two episodes of the television show _[Family Matters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Family_Matters "Family Matters")_: "Robo-Nerd" and "Robo-Nerd II".

Chambers was the announcer on the children's show _[Fun House](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fun_House_(U.S._game_show) "Fun House (U.S. game show)")_ from 1990 to 1991. He also appeared in [Sugar Ray](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sugar_Ray "Sugar Ray")'s 1997 video for the song "[Fly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fly_(Sugar_Ray_song) "Fly (Sugar Ray song)")" and [break danced](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Break_dancing "Break dancing") in the video.

Chambers has been invited as a speaker several times to USC's [Thorton School of Music](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USC_Thornton_School_of_Music "USC Thornton School of Music"). He has also spoken at [Santa Monica College](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Santa_Monica_College "Santa Monica College"), the [University of Redlands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Redlands "University of Redlands"), and gave a surprise visit to [Spelman College](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spelman_College "Spelman College") in Georgia, U.S.

In 2014, Chambers was in talks to do a second sequel to the _Breakin'_ franchise. As of 2020, Chambers is set to release a website, as well as music and YouTube pages, featuring his original footage from his early career.
